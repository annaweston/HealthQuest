Paroxysmal Atrial Fibrillation

In this type of AF, the abnormal heart rhythm starts and stops spontaneously, without any treatments (such as taking a drug or having a cardioversion).

In this situation, it may be best to try to keep your heart in normal rhythm and try to stop atrial fibrillation from occurring – called a “rhythm control strategy”.

This may require medication, a cardioversion, and/or atrial fibrillation ablation treatments.

Persistent atrial fibrillation

In this type of AF, once the abnormal heart rhythm has started it will not return to normal rhythm unless the individual has some treatment – this could be a drug or a cardioversion.

In this situation, it may be best to try to keep your heart in normal rhythm and try to stop atrial fibrillation from occurring – called a “rhythm control strategy”.

This may require medication, a cardioversion, and/or atrial fibrillation ablation treatments.

Permanent atrial fibrillation

In this type of AF, the abnormal heart rhythm cannot be stopped and normal heart rhythm can never be achieved, because the electrical systems in the atrium have changed too much.

In this situation, to control the symptoms that permanent AF causes, you may employ a “rate control strategy”.

This may involve drugs and sometimes a “nodal” ablation and pacemaker implant.
